Public Restroom Redesign

Human Factors involves the study of factors and the development of tools that facilitate the goals of enhancing performance, increasing safety, and augmenting user satisfaction.

The objective of this project was to find and fix something that involves real-world human factors problems.

Project Overview

Course Title: Human Factors
Project Type: Individual
Duration: 13 weeks
Methods: think-alouds, task analysis

Why Public Restrooms?

Researchers have found that 15% of the American population routinely fails to wash their hands after using a public restroom. Another 30% of the population avoids public restrooms completely.

Health consequences are thought to arise from two distinct sources: (1) bacterial/viral infections acquired through contact with unsanitary fixtures, and (2) feelings of anxiety, fear, and/or disgust associated with the use of public restrooms. In fact, the latter has been linked to other health conditions such as urinary infections and social isolation.

Task Analysis

The following steps reflect one correct way to perform the hand washing task in the evaluated setting.

  1. Ensure paper towel is accessible

  2. Set up washing environment

    1. Find an available sink

    2. Turn on water

    3. Test and adjust water temperature

  3. Wash hands

    1. Briefly place hands under running water

    2. Dispense soap

    3. Rub hands together for ≥ 20 seconds

      1. Wash palms, between fingers, back of hands, base of thumbs, back of fingers, under fingernails, and wrists

    4. Rinse hands with water

  4. Dry hands with paper towel

  5. Turn off water faucet using paper towel

  6. Exit restroom

    1. Pull door handle using paper towel

    2. Discard paper towel in an appropriate waste receptacle outside

User Study

Think-aloud studies with three female participants were conducted to witness performance of the hand washing task and identify possible sources of task execution errors, mistakes, and inefficiencies.

User Errors

  • Not following the procedure outlined by the CDC

  • Becoming re-contaminated after washing

  • Cross-contaminating clothing and belongings

  • Not dispensing enough soap

Maintenance Issues

  • Broken faucet handle

  • Damaged paper towel dispenser

Design Oversights

  • Lid on the trash receptacle

  • Location of the trash receptacle

Inefficiencies

  • Users must dispense paper towel prior to hand washing

  • Multiple steps are required to adjust the water temperature

Redesign

Amenities

  1. Sinks with foot pedals

  2. Automatic soap dispensers

  3. Lights that remain illuminated for the recommended duration of hand washing (activated by dispensing soap)

  4. Displays that outline the correct hand washing procedure

  5. Automatic paper towel dispenser

  6. Automatic exit door
