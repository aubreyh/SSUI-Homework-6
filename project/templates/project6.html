Breakfast Delivery Service

The goal of this project was to create a mobile service concept and assess its technical feasibility, financial viability, and desirability.

Project Overview

Course Title: Mobile Service Innovation
Project Type: Team
Duration: 14 weeks
Methods: observation, interviews, surveys, competitive analysis, speed dating, branding, A/B testing, prototypes

Problem

31 million Americans (~10% of the population) skip breakfast each morning. This practice has been linked with increased risk of obesity, diabetes, high blood pressure, and heart attack.

Research

Data collected from 11 interview subjects and 59 survey respondents revealed the following key findings.

Interviews

Customers

  • Want fresh food fast, with little additional cost

  • Meal routines change depending on day of week and time of day

Restaurants

  • Typically partner with several delivery services

  • Offering fewer menu options facilitates meal preparation

Surveys

  • Breakfast delivery is an untapped market

  • Professionals are much more likely to eat breakfast in the morning than students

  • Customers are willing to tolerate limited menu options

  • When busy, tired, or at work, many people would prefer having food delivered (as opposed to cooking or dining out)

Speed Dating

Speed Dating is a design method for rapidly exploring application concepts, their interactions, and contextual dimensions.

This method was applied during both in-person and online sessions (24 total) with key stakeholders to help determine which idea to pursue.

Findings

Participants

  • Responded best to the customizable meal concept

  • Expressed concern about meal dropoff, where food can get cold or stolen

  • Were willing to pay $3-$5 per use of the breakfast delivery service

  • Want in-advance and on-demand ordering

  • Desire more menu options/variety

  • Preferred in-person delivery to drop-off (83%)

  • Would use the breakfast delivery service 2x / week, and pay a 10-20% delivery fee

Outcome

After considering several factors (user need, popularity, revenue, and cost), we decided to pursue the breakfast delivery concept.

Branding

To create a brand identity that would capture the essence of our service concept, we explored different ideas for the name, color scheme, typography, iconography, and catch phrase.
