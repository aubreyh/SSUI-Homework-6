Health Management System

This project focused on designing technological solutions to assist
in-home caregivers.

Project Overview

Course Title: Rapid Prototyping of Computer Systems
Project Type: Team
Duration: 15 weeks
Methods: interviews, surveys, contextual design, persona creation, prototypes, heuristic evaluation, wireframes, usability testing

Problem

Currently, 19% of the U.S. population act as informal caregivers. That percentage is expected to increase to 50% by the year 2030. Due to the stressful and demanding nature of their role, caregivers are at an increased risk of acquiring many physical and psychological health conditions.

Conceptualization

Process

1. Interviewed six caregivers
2. Distilled key issues & insights
3. Created flow models
4. Constructed affinity diagram
5. Held visioning session
6. Defined functional     requirements

Proposed Solution

A health management system that helps caregivers track patients’ vital signs and organize pertinent medical information.

How it Works

Design

Preliminary mockups

Paper prototypes

Heuristic Evaluation

Evaluators

20 Participants

  • Students

  • 13 males, 8 females

  • Average age – 24

  • Randomly assigned to 1 of 5 conditions

Assessment

Each design was scored on the System Usability Scale (SUS).

Results

Functional Prototype

This click-through prototype incorporated the highest scoring features of each design.

Usability Studies

Pilot Testing

5 Participants

  • CMU faculty

  • 4 males, 1 female

  • Average age – 67


Results

Addressed 14/15 identified usability issues.

Field Testing

6 Participants

  • Assisted living facility residents/staff

  • All female

  • Average age – 69.5


Results

Addressed 2/2 identified usability issues.

Discovered that prior experience with technology is required to use the system.

Wireframes

Several modifications to the design were made to reflect the feedback obtained from usability testing.
